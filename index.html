About

I am a PhD candidate at the College of Environmental Sciences and Engineering, Peking University.

My research focuses on understanding how river water quality and material transport respond to climate change and human activities. Using satellite remote sensing, machine learning, hydrological data, and spatial analysis, I reconstruct long-term dynamics of suspended sediment, particulate organic carbon, and other water-quality indicators from individual river reaches to regional and global river networks.

My current research covers rivers across High Mountain Asia, the pan-Arctic region, China, and the globe. A central goal of my work is to bridge the gap between sparse field observations and large-scale river monitoring, and to better understand the roles of rivers in sediment transport, the land-ocean continuum, and the global carbon cycle.

💻Datasets

Global River POC (1984-2018)

This dataset provides satellite-constrained annual particulate organic carbon fluxes for 2,409 global rivers from 1984 to 2018, together with river-mouth attributes and a web-based visualization interface for exploring long-term changes in riverine carbon export.

Dataset website: Interactive visualization

Download: Zenodo dataset

Repository: GitHub repository

Arctic River SSC (1984-2023)

This dataset provides reach-scale suspended sediment concentration records for 4,331 river reaches across the pan-Arctic region. The visualization groups mean SSC into 8 quantile classes and links mapped reaches with 5-year SSC time series from 1984-1988 to 2019-2023.

Dataset website: Interactive visualization

Download: Zenodo dataset

Paper: Nature Geoscience
