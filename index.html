About

I am a PhD candidate in Dongfeng Li's lab at the College of Environmental Sciences and Engineering, Peking University.

My research centers on investigating long-term changes in river sediment, carbon, and other water-quality constituents across river networks in High Mountain Asia, the pan-Arctic region, and worldwide. By integrating satellite remote sensing, machine learning, hydrological observations, and other multimodal data, I seek to understand how river systems respond to climate change and human activities. My broader goal is to reveal how river networks regulate the redistribution of water, sediment, carbon, and nutrients across the Earth system, and to advance a predictive understanding of their responses and feedbacks to global environmental change.

💻Datasets

Global River POC (1984-2018)

This dataset provides satellite-constrained annual particulate organic carbon fluxes for 2,409 global rivers from 1984 to 2018, together with a web-based visualization interface for exploring long-term changes in riverine carbon export.

Dataset website: Interactive visualization

Arctic River SSC (1984-2023)
